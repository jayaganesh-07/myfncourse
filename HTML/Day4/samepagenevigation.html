The 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly Election created a major change in the state’s political history. Vijay’s party, Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK), emerged as the single largest party by winning 108 seats, while the DMK alliance secured 73 seats and the AIADMK alliance won 53 seats. Since no party achieved the required majority of 118 seats, the election resulted in a hung assembly. The results marked a historic shift because Tamil Nadu politics had mainly been dominated by the DMK and AIADMK for several decades. About the Tn Election

The Tamil Nadu Assembly Election is an important democratic process in which people vote to choose their representatives and form the state government. Elections are conducted every five years, and major political parties such as the DMK, AIADMK, and other regional parties compete to win seats in the Legislative Assembly. The party that secures the majority of seats forms the government, and its leader becomes the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. The election plays a major role in deciding the development, welfare schemes, education, employment, and future policies of the state. People across Tamil Nadu actively participate in voting, making the election an important part of democracy.

Result

The 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly Election results brought a major change in the political history of the state. Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK), led by Vijay, became the single largest party by winning 108 seats. The DMK alliance secured 73 seats, while the AIADMK alliance won 53 seats. Since no party crossed the majority mark of 118 seats, the election resulted in a hung assembly. The results were widely discussed because Tamil Nadu politics had been mainly dominated by the DMK and AIADMK for many decades. After the election, political parties began alliance discussions and government formation talks, making the election an important turning point in Tamil Nadu politics.
